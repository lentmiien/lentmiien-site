# Research and source notes

Research was checked on 2026-07-23. The experience uses rounded ages and child-friendly language, but preserves uncertainty where the evidence is incomplete.

## Geological clock

The [International Commission on Stratigraphy’s December 2024 chart](https://stratigraphy.org/ICSchart/ChronostratChart2024-12.pdf) is the timeline authority used here.

- Triassic begins at 251.902 ± 0.024 million years ago and ends at 201.4 ± 0.2 million years ago.
- Jurassic ends at 143.1 ± 0.6 million years ago.
- The Cretaceous–Paleogene boundary is 66.00 million years ago.

The UI rounds those to 252–201, 201–143, and 143–66 million years ago. Many otherwise authoritative museum learning pages still use 145 million years for the Jurassic–Cretaceous boundary. Numerical ages are revised as dating improves; formally defined rock boundary points remain the standard. The narration therefore says “about” and the help dialog explains that dates and known fossil ranges can change.

## Chapter evidence map

### 1. Deep time

- [Natural History Museum: When did dinosaurs live?](https://www.nhm.ac.uk/discover/when-did-dinosaurs-live.html)
- [Fukui Prefectural Dinosaur Museum: 恐竜が生きていた時代の名前は？](https://www.dinosaur.pref.fukui.jp/dino/faq/r02015.html)
- International Chronostratigraphic Chart above

The three-period structure is established. Pterosaurs and marine reptiles are deliberately separated from Dinosauria.

### 2. Triassic beginnings

- [Natural History Museum: Where did dinosaurs come from?](https://www.nhm.ac.uk/discover/where-did-dinosaurs-come-from.html)
- [American Museum of Natural History: Triassic Period](https://www.amnh.org/explore/ology/ology-cards/262-triassic-period)

The narration uses “earliest well-established dinosaurs around 230 million years ago.” It does not claim a single undisputed first dinosaur. The image’s skin colors and social grouping are artistic choices.

### 3. Jurassic giants

- [Natural History Museum: When did Diplodocus live?](https://www.nhm.ac.uk/discover/quick-questions/when-did-diplodocus-live.html)
- [Natural History Museum: Stegosaurus](https://www.nhm.ac.uk/discover/dino-directory/Stegosaurus.html)

The museum ranges used in the species comparison are Diplodocus 155–145 and Stegosaurus 152–145 million years ago. Their fossils occur in Late Jurassic North America. The principal function of Stegosaurus plates remains uncertain.

### 4. Feathers and birds

- [Natural History Museum: How did birds and other dinosaurs learn to fly?](https://www.nhm.ac.uk/discover/how-did-birds-and-other-dinosaurs-learn-to-fly.html)
- [American Museum of Natural History: Figuring Out Feathers](https://www.amnh.org/exhibitions/dinosaurs-ancient-fossils/liaoning-diorama/figuring-out-feathers)
- [Smithsonian Institution: Could dinosaurs still exist?](https://www.si.edu/stories/could-dinosaurs-still-exist)

The story says many—not all—dinosaurs had feathers. Archaeopteryx is described as a bird-like mosaic rather than an uncontested “first bird.” Its flight ability is marked as debated.

### 5. Cretaceous worlds and Spinosaurus

- [Natural History Museum: Spinosaurus](https://www.nhm.ac.uk/discover/dino-directory/spinosaurus.html)
- [Natural History Museum: When did dinosaurs live?](https://www.nhm.ac.uk/discover/when-did-dinosaurs-live.html)

The species range used is 99–93.5 million years ago in North Africa. Fish eating is supported by jaw and tooth form. How much it swam, its exact posture, and its movement on land remain active research questions, so the artwork places it wading near shore rather than asserting a high-speed swimming behavior.

### 6. Gobi life and Velociraptor

- [Natural History Museum: Velociraptor](https://www.nhm.ac.uk/discover/dino-directory/velociraptor.html)
- [American Museum of Natural History: Dinosaur Eggs](https://www.amnh.org/dinosaurs/dinosaur-eggs)

The experience uses the museum’s 74–70-million-year range, 1.8-meter length, approximately 7-kilogram mass, Mongolia location, and feather-like covering. Nest behavior is phrased as possible evidence rather than a universal parenting rule.

### 7. Last neighbors

- [Natural History Museum: Tyrannosaurus](https://www.nhm.ac.uk/discover/dino-directory/tyrannosaurus.html)
- [Natural History Museum: Triceratops](https://www.nhm.ac.uk/discover/dino-directory/triceratops.html)
- [Natural History Museum: Stegosaurus](https://www.nhm.ac.uk/discover/dino-directory/Stegosaurus.html)
- [Natural History Museum: Velociraptor](https://www.nhm.ac.uk/discover/dino-directory/velociraptor.html)
- [Smithsonian National Museum of Natural History: The Last American Dinosaurs](https://naturalhistory.si.edu/exhibits/last-american-dinosaurs-discovering-lost-world)

Tyrannosaurus rex and Triceratops overlap at 68–66 million years ago in western North America. Stegosaurus ends at least 77 million years before the known Tyrannosaurus range. Velociraptor’s known range ends earlier and is in Mongolia. These are comparisons of currently known fossils; a new oldest or youngest find could move an edge.

### 8. Fossil evidence

- [Natural History Museum: What can scientists learn about dinosaurs?](https://www.nhm.ac.uk/discover/what-can-scientists-learn-about-dinosaurs-and-how.html)
- [Natural History Museum: Dinosaur footprints](https://www.nhm.ac.uk/discover/dinosaur-footprints.html)

Body fossils, trace fossils, trackway interpretation, growth lines, and reconstruction methods are established. Most colors and sounds remain unknown. The field excavation artwork is a generated documentary-style scene, not a record of a particular dig.

### 9. Chicxulub and survival

- [Natural History Museum: What killed the dinosaurs?](https://www.nhm.ac.uk/discover/dinosaur-extinction.html)
- [NASA Earth Observatory: Sediment Swirls Off the Yucatán](https://science.nasa.gov/earth/earth-observatory/sediment-swirls-off-the-yucatan-149114/)
- [Smithsonian Institution: Could dinosaurs still exist?](https://www.si.edu/stories/could-dinosaurs-still-exist)

Strong evidence identifies the Chicxulub impact as the primary trigger of the end-Cretaceous mass extinction. NASA’s accessible estimates—about a 14-kilometer impactor, a roughly 180-kilometer crater, and around 75 percent of species lost—are used. The script notes that enormous volcanism was also changing climate, and avoids claiming every animal died at the instant of impact. All non-bird dinosaur lineages disappeared; surviving bird lineages carried Dinosauria forward.

## Reconstruction policy

- Every prehistoric image is disclosed as an artist’s reconstruction.
- No scene is presented as a documentary photograph.
- Colors, sounds, soft tissues, and most individual behaviors are treated as uncertain.
- Adult Tyrannosaurus is shown mostly scaled, consistent with preserved skin evidence; the experience does not claim that feather extent is completely known.
- Velociraptor is shown small and feathered.
- Spinosaurus is shown near shore because its swimming and terrestrial locomotion remain disputed.
- Coexisting animals are chosen from compatible known ranges and regions. Quiz callbacks never introduce a mixed-era “dinosaur crowd.”
