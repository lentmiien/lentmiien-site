Stand at the doorway and take one enormous step backward. The Mesozoic Era began about two hundred fifty-two million years ago and ended sixty-six million years ago. That is nearly one hundred eighty-six million years of change. Scientists divide it into three great periods: the Triassic, the Jurassic, and the Cretaceous. Continents moved. Oceans opened. Forests changed. Dinosaur families appeared and disappeared. So, there was never one cast of dinosaurs sharing one stage. Stegosaurus did not watch Tyrannosaurus walk past, and no human ever saw a non-bird dinosaur alive. Keep that time compass ready. Our first stop is a hot, dry world called Pangaea.
