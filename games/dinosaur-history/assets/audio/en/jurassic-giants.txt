Welcome to the Jurassic. Pangaea is pulling apart, rain is reaching more land, and fern plains spread beneath conifer forests. Plant eaters have an enormous buffet. Diplodocus sweeps leaves with peg-like teeth, then swallows without chewing. Nearby, Stegosaurus carries tall plates in its skin and powerful spikes on its tail. Their known fossil ranges overlap in Late Jurassic North America, so these famous dinosaurs really could have shared a landscape. Scientists are still testing what the plates did. They may have helped with display, recognition, or temperature control. Even a giant, obvious-looking feature can keep a mystery for one hundred fifty million years.
