Look closely at those wings. Archaeopteryx, pronounced ar-kee-OP-ter-ix, had long feathers, but also teeth, clawed fingers, and a long bony tail. It lived around one hundred fifty million years ago. Many non-bird dinosaurs wore feathers too, although not every dinosaur did. The first feathers probably helped with warmth, display, or camouflage before some became useful for flight. Scientists still debate how strongly Archaeopteryx could fly. What is clear is the family connection: birds evolved inside a branch of small, two-legged theropod dinosaurs. A feather was not a sudden invention. It was an old tool that evolution kept reshaping.
