Now the wind carries sand across Mongolia, much later in the Cretaceous. Meet the real Velociraptor: about one point eight meters from nose to tail and roughly seven kilograms, with a fine feathered covering. It was far smaller than the movie monster people often imagine. Around it, eggs and nests hold another kind of story. Every dinosaur known to science hatched from an egg, including today’s birds. Fossil nests can show how eggs were arranged. Embryos reveal growth, and adults found near nests may offer clues about care. These are precious snapshots, not complete movies, so paleontologists say what the evidence shows—and mark the rest as a careful possibility.
