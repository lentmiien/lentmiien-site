The Cretaceous was not one single world. It lasted about seventy-seven million years while continents separated, shallow seas spread, and flowering plants became more common. Here in North Africa, around ninety-six million years ago, Spinosaurus waits beside a tidal river. Its long snout and cone-shaped teeth were well suited to catching fish. New fossils revealed short legs and a broad, paddle-like tail, changing old pictures of this animal. But the case is not closed. Researchers still debate how much time Spinosaurus spent swimming and exactly how it moved on land. A reconstruction is a best explanation built from today’s clues, ready to change when tomorrow brings another bone.
