Morning returns to the canyon, sixty-six million years later. The great non-bird dinosaurs are gone, but Dinosauria is not. Birds are living dinosaurs, descended from the small feathered theropod branch. Watch a pigeon walk on two legs. Notice a crow’s scaly feet, curved claws, hollow bones, and feathers. These are not costumes copied from dinosaurs. They are parts of the same continuing family history. Our journey crossed three periods, many continents, and countless changing species. Its best lesson is simple: ask when, ask where, and follow the clues. Then listen outside. Every chirp is a small echo from the Mesozoic.
