The dinosaur story begins quietly. Around two hundred thirty million years ago, the earliest well-established dinosaurs were mostly small, quick animals on two legs. Nearly all the land was joined into one supercontinent, Pangaea. Its vast interior could be hot and dry. Early dinosaurs were not yet the rulers. Larger crocodile relatives and many other reptiles shared the ground. Then, near the Triassic ending, a mass extinction removed many competing groups. The dinosaurs that survived found new opportunities. Over millions of years, their descendants would become armored browsers, long-necked giants, hunters, and feathered fliers. Every giant story can begin with a small set of footprints.
