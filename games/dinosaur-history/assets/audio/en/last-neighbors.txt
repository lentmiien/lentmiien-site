At last, we reach western North America between about sixty-eight and sixty-six million years ago. Tyrannosaurus rex and Triceratops really were neighbors here. Their known fossil ranges overlap in both time and place. Stegosaurus, however, had disappeared at least seventy-seven million years before Tyrannosaurus appeared. Velociraptor lived earlier too, and on another continent. Here is the amazing time twist: Tyrannosaurus lived closer to us than it did to Stegosaurus. All those names may stand together in a toy box, but the rocks place them on very different pages. The fossil range is our calendar, and a new discovery can still adjust its edges.
