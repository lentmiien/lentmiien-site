# Generated event artwork

These nine images were generated with the built-in image-generation tool, then converted to 1600×900 WebP at quality 84 for the simulation. All prompts requested wide, text-free, watermark-free artist impressions with a scientifically grounded visual direction.

## `solar-nebula.webp`

Use case: scientific-educational. Cinematic full-screen interstitial depicting the Solar System’s birth about 4.6 billion years ago: a young protostar inside a broad rotating protoplanetary disk of gas, dust, glowing mineral grains, and forming planetesimals. Photorealistic astronomical concept art, low sweeping 16:9 view, amber-white protostar and ember-gold dust against graphite space. No mature planets, spacecraft, people, labels, text, logos, fantasy galaxies, or perfect rings.

## `moon-forming-impact.webp`

Use case: scientific-educational. The leading giant-impact hypothesis roughly 4.5 billion years ago: a Mars-sized protoplanet colliding obliquely with the hot young Earth and ejecting incandescent rocky debris into orbit. Wide three-quarter view with a curved debris trail, molten basalt and white-hot impact light. Young Earth has no blue oceans or modern continents; no intact modern Moon, labels, text, or exploding planet.
